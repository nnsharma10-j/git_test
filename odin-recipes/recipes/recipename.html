Paneer Kadai

Certainly! Paneer Kadai is a popular Indian vegetarian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a spicy tomato-based gravy with bell peppers.
"Kadai" refers to an Indian wok, but any deep pan can be used to make this dish.

Paneer Kadai Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • Paneer - 250 grams (cubed)
  • Capsicum/bell peppers - 1 large (diced)
  • Tomatoes - 3-4 medium-sized (pureed)
  • Onions - 2 medium-sized (finely chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
  • Green chili - 1 (slit)
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) - 1 tbsp
  • Red chili powder - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Coriander powder - 1 tsp
  • Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
  • Fresh cream - 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Oil or ghee - 2-3 tbsp
  • Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Paneer Preparation:

If you're using store-bought paneer, soak the cubes in warm water for about 10 minutes to soften them. Drain and set aside.

Cooking the Gravy:

Heat oil or ghee in a kadai or pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add finely chopped onions and sauté till they become translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste and the slit green chili. Sauté till the raw smell goes away. Add the tomato puree and mix well. Cook until oil starts to separate from the masala. Add the red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes. Add the diced bell peppers and cook for another 2-3 minutes, so they remain slightly crunchy. Add the prepared Kadai masala and mix well. Add paneer cubes and gently mix ensuring the cubes are well-coated with the masala. If the gravy seems too thick, you can add some water to achieve the desired consistency. Add garam masala and kasuri methi. If using, also add the fresh cream and mix well. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Serve hot with roti, naan, or any Indian bread. Enjoy your homemade Paneer Kadai!
